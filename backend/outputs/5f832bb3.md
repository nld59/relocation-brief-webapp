# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels, with a budget of 490000–895000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, expat community, and value for money.

## Must-have
- Minimum 1 bedroom apartment
- Access to public transportation
- Proximity to expat community
- Nearby cafes and brunch spots
- Reliable internet connectivity

## Nice-to-have
- Balcony or outdoor space
- Modern kitchen facilities
- Parking availability
- Proximity to parks
- Cultural venues nearby

## Red flags
- High property taxes in certain areas
- Limited inventory in popular neighborhoods
- Potential noise from busy streets
- Unfavorable building regulations
- Long wait times for property transactions

## Trade-offs
- Modern amenities vs. historical character
- Central location vs. budget constraints
- Large living space vs. proximity to cafes
- New construction vs. established neighborhoods

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Great cafe culture around Flagey Square attracts diverse international residents.
- Vibrant community events and expat activities foster social connections.

**Watch-out:**
- Rental prices can spike due to popularity and demand.

### 2) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Charming streets with brunch spots encourage a vibrant expat lifestyle.
- Cultural diversity offers numerous international community events weekly.

**Watch-out:**
- Noise levels may increase during festivals and events.

### 3) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Peaceful parks like Parc de Woluwe provide an escape from urban life.
- Family-friendly environment supports child-friendly amenities and schools.

**Watch-out:**
- Commutes can be longer due to distance from city center.
