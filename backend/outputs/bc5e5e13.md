# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels. Budget is 490,000–895,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes & brunch, expats & international, and value for money.

## Must-have
- 1+ room apartment
- Access to public transport
- Near cafes & brunch spots
- Safe neighborhood
- Good resale potential

## Nice-to-have
- Proximity to parks
- Community amenities
- Expats community features
- Low maintenance charges
- Balcony or outdoor space

## Red flags
- High property taxes
- Unsafe neighborhoods
- Limited parking options
- Poor internet connectivity
- Short lease terms in certain buildings

## Trade-offs
- Modern amenities versus historic charm
- Budget limitations versus location preference
- Commute convenience versus lifestyle richness
- Space versus location proximity

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:3 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Vibrant expat community anchored by Flagey square and countless brunch cafes.
- Easy transit connections via the multiple tram lines serving the area.

**Watch-out:**
- Property prices may stretch the budget due to high demand.

### 2) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Known for its cultural diversity, with weekly markets and various brunch spots.
- Metro station offers quick access to the city center and beyond.

**Watch-out:**
- Some noisy areas may affect living quality, especially at night.

### 3) Woluwe-Saint-Lambert
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Family-friendly district featuring parks and affordable cafes for brunch outings.
- Major bus and tram lines provide direct links to central Brussels.

**Watch-out:**
- Potential for budget stretch due to family-oriented amenities.


## Next steps
- Schedule property viewings in top districts.
- Consult with agencies about available listings.
- Review property paperwork and legal requirements.
- Assess financing options based on budget.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What are the total costs associated with the purchase?
- Are there any legal compliance issues to be aware of?
- What are the building charges or upcoming works planned?
- What is the condition of the property and how can I inspect it?
- What is the expected timeline for this transaction?
