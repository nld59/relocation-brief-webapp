# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple looking to buy a 2+ room apartment in Brussels. Budget is 660000-1120000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, restaurants, and a quiet residential area.

## Must-have
- Proximity to cafes and brunch spots
- Access to good restaurants
- Quiet residential environment
- Minimum of 2 bedrooms in apartment

## Nice-to-have
- Access to public transport
- Nearby parks for relaxation
- Vibrant neighborhood for young professionals

## Red flags
- Potential for traffic noise in busy districts
- High property taxes in premium areas
- Market may be competitive for quality listings

## Trade-offs
- Quiet areas may lack vibrant nightlife
- Cafes and brunch may not align with quiet living
- High demand may push prices beyond budget in popular areas

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Boondael is known for its green parks and local cafes.
- Flagey offers lively restaurants and strong public transport access.

**Watch-out:**
- Traffic in busy areas makes parking challenging and rents higher.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Boondael offers green spaces and a lively cafe scene at Châtelain. (_watch-out_: Popular streets get noisy on weekends; parking is often tight.)
- **Brugmann - Lepoutre** — Brugmann features charming parks and excellent restaurants for dining. (_watch-out_: Traffic noise can arise during peak hours around major routes.)
- **Universite** — Universite is vibrant, attracting young professionals and expats. (_watch-out_: Nightlife venues can create noise disturbances late at night.)

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Etterbeek has parks and cultural spots, making it family-friendly.
- It's well-connected to public transport routes and cafes.

**Watch-out:**
- Traffic can increase around major streets, affecting quiet living.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — This area is lively, offering good cafes and easy transit options. (_watch-out_: Rents near EU quarter can be higher; traffic around main axes.)
- **Saint - Pierre** — Saint-Pierre features a vibrant local atmosphere with amenities nearby. (_watch-out_: Increased demand may lead to competitive pricing and higher costs.)
- **Saint - Michel** — Saint-Michel provides access to parks and quality cafes for brunch. (_watch-out_: Busy streets can make quiet living difficult in some pockets.)

### 3) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Strong fit for your priorities around Parvis de Saint-Gilles.
- Balanced trade-off between lifestyle and commute.

**Watch-out:**
- Some streets are lively and noisy late; choose quieter side streets.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Bas Forest** — Good starting point with good tram coverage. (_watch-out_: Watch for less greenery.)
- **Haut Saint - Gilles** — Good starting point with more green space, active cafe scene, good metro access. (_watch-out_: Watch for busier evenings.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
