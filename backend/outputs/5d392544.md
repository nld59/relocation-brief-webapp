# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family buying a house in Brussels. Budget is 490,000–895,000 EUR. Priorities include parks, cafes, and family-friendly areas.

## Must-have
- Minimum 2 bedrooms and 1 bathroom.
- Access to parks within 15 min walking distance.
- Proximity to international schools for children.
- Quiet residential neighborhood for family.
- Public transport accessibility for commuting.

## Nice-to-have
- Nearby cafes and brunch spots for family outings.
- Green spaces for recreational activities.
- Community of expats for social integration.
- Restaurants offering diverse international cuisine.
- Child-friendly amenities and play areas.

## Red flags
- High noise levels from nearby commercial areas.
- Properties with less than 5 years warranty.
- Poor maintenance history of the house.
- Budget constraints limiting housing options.
- Schools rated below average in the district.

## Trade-offs
- Desire for a quiet area yet looking for vibrant cafes.
- Need for parks but budget may limit location choices.
- Interest in expat community may restrict school options.
- High budget expectations may conflict with quiet neighborhoods.

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Enjoy parks like Flagey Square that are perfect for families.
- Rich expat community offers multiple international cafes and restaurants.

**Watch-out:**
- Rental prices may stretch your budget due to high demand.

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Close proximity to Parc du Cinquantenaire provides great outdoor activities.
- Family-friendly vibe attracts many expat families and community events.

**Watch-out:**
- Commute times can be longer due to traffic during peak hours.

### 3) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Features many parks like Parc de Wolvendael for family enjoyment.
- Quiet residential streets and a strong community feel enhance family living.

**Watch-out:**
- Distance from city center may affect commute options.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What is the deposit and any additional fees required?
- Are utilities included in the rental price?
- What is the lease term and termination process?
- Are there any noise restrictions or rules?
- Is there an indexation clause in the rental agreement?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- What distance is acceptable for commuting to school?
- Are there specific neighborhoods you prefer?
- Any preferences regarding property age or condition?
