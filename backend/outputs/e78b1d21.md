# Relocation Brief — Brussels

## Client profile
Family relocating to Brussels, looking to buy a home. Budget is 730,000–1,090,000 EUR, prioritizing restaurants, parks, expat community, and central access.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms in a family-friendly neighborhood
- Proximity to restaurants and parks
- Access to schools within 20 min walk
- Strong public transportation options
- Expats community for social integration

## Nice-to-have
- Access to childcare facilities
- Family-oriented activities and events
- Quiet residential streets
- Local markets for fresh produce
- Cafes for family gatherings

## Red flags
- Higher rents near central business districts
- Limited availability of suitable properties
- Potentially noisy environments in tourist spots
- Competition from other expat families
- Budget challenges in highly desirable areas

## Trade-offs
- Prefer central yet quiet locations
- Desire for parks but wary of noise
- Want community yet need privacy
- Seeking proximity to transit while avoiding traffic

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Close to Cinquantenaire park, great for family outings.
- Strong schools nearby, fostering community and support.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near EU quarter can be higher and traffic can be busy.

### 2) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Châtelain area offers vibrant markets and family-friendly parks.
- Strong expat community creates a welcoming atmosphere for newcomers.

**Watch-out:**
- Popular streets get noisy on weekends; consider quieter options.

### 3) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:3 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Proximity to Royal Palace park enhances recreational opportunities.
- Cultural attractions enrich family life, striking a balance between urban and green.

**Watch-out:**
- Touristy areas can be noisy, especially during weekends.


## Next steps
- Research neighborhoods based on preferences.
- Contact real estate agencies for listings.
- Schedule property viewings in shortlisted areas.
- Gather necessary documents for purchase process.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What are the total costs involved in the purchase?
- Are there any legal compliance issues I should know?
- What are the building charges or upcoming works?
- What is the current condition of the property?
- What is the expected timeline for negotiation and closing?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- What is the preferred property type (apartment/house)?
- Are there specific school districts of interest?
- What is the desired timeline for the move?
