# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels, with a budget of 490,000–895,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes & brunch, family-friendly amenities, and expat community.

## Must-have
- 1+ room apartment in safe areas
- Access to quality schools within 15 min
- Nearby parks for family activities
- Public transport access for ease of commute
- Strong expat community in the district

## Nice-to-have
- Local cafes and brunch spots nearby
- Proximity to cultural amenities
- Quiet streets with less traffic
- Walkable neighborhoods for daily errands
- Outdoor spaces for children to play

## Red flags
- High living costs could complicate budgets
- Limited availability of family-friendly apartments
- Noise levels in certain districts may be concerning
- Commute times could be longer in busy areas
- Future developments might alter neighborhood dynamics

## Trade-offs
- Desire for quiet areas vs. cafe accessibility
- Family-friendly requirements vs. urban vibrancy
- Preference for expat community vs. local integration
- Budget limitations vs. desired neighborhood quality

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Cafes around Flagey Square attract a vibrant community and offer diverse dining options.
- Schools like St. Boniface provide excellent education, making Ixelles family-friendly.

**Watch-out:**
- High demand may lead to competitive pricing and quick sales in preferred areas.

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Parc du Cinquantenaire offers green spaces, ideal for families and outdoor activities.
- Good international schools cater to expat families, fostering community connections.

**Watch-out:**
- Potential noise from nearby traffic may impact peaceful living experiences.

### 3) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Cafes and brunch spots in the area create a lively atmosphere for socializing.
- The presence of diverse schools appeals to families and expats alike.

**Watch-out:**
- Rental prices are rising, making properties less affordable for families.
