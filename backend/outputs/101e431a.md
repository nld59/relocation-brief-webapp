# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels with a budget of 660000–1120000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, restaurants, and a quiet residential area.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms in apartment
- Quiet residential area
- Proximity to cafes and restaurants

## Nice-to-have
- Good public transport access
- Green spaces nearby
- Vibrant local market

## Red flags
- Possibility of noise in busy areas
- Parking can be limited in some districts
- Higher costs near popular cafes

## Trade-offs
- Desiring a quiet area while close to vibrant cafes
- Budget stretch for upscale neighborhoods
- Mixing residential quiet with trendy nightlife

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Charming local cafes near Châtelain and Flagey squares enhance community feel.
- Strengthening transport links make commutes to the city center manageable.

**Watch-out:**
- Popular streets get noisy on weekends; parking is often tight.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Known for its greenery and lively atmosphere, ideal for couples. (_watch-out_: Busy streets can mean noise and tight parking.)
- **Brugmann - Lepoutre** — Offers a peaceful ambiance and ample cafes nearby. (_watch-out_: Traffic can be heavy during peak hours.)
- **Universite** — Great for transit access and vibrant cafes for young professionals. (_watch-out_: Can be noisy during university events.)

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Exceptional park access at Cinquantenaire enhances outdoor activities.
- Central location provides convenient commuting options across Brussels.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near EU quarter can be higher; traffic around main axes.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — A lively area with great access to cafes and parks. (_watch-out_: Near EU quarter, rents can be higher.)
- **Chasse** — Known for vibrant cafés and community activities. (_watch-out_: Traffic can lead to congestion and noise.)
- **Saint - Pierre** — Offers a blend of parks and eateries ideal for relaxation. (_watch-out_: Busy roads can impact quietness.)

### 3) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Strong fit for your priorities around Parvis de Saint-Gilles.
- Balanced trade-off between lifestyle and commute.

**Watch-out:**
- Some streets are lively and noisy late; choose quieter side streets.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Bas Forest** — Good starting point with good tram coverage. (_watch-out_: Watch for less greenery.)
- **Haut Saint - Gilles** — Good starting point with more green space, active cafe scene, good metro access. (_watch-out_: Watch for busier evenings.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
