# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels. Budget is 725000–1325000 EUR. Priorities include cafes & brunch, restaurants, a premium feel, and a residential environment.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms available
- Premium feel throughout
- Residential and quiet atmosphere

## Nice-to-have
- Access to good transit options
- Local cafes and restaurants nearby

## Red flags
- High competition for premium listings
- Potentially long commute from some districts

## Trade-offs
- Premium feel may conflict with budget limits
- Desire for quiet areas may limit access to restaurants

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Vibrant area with access to parks like Flagey.
- Diverse dining options attract both locals and tourists.

**Watch-out:**
- Expect some street noise on weekends due to nightlife.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Offers a green environment and lively scene with parks. (_watch-out_: Popular streets can experience significant noise and tight parking.)
- **Universite** — Known for lively atmosphere, good transit, and vibrant cafes. (_watch-out_: Can be expensive, especially for premium apartments.)

### 2) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Charming streets lined with cafes close to Parvis.
- Excellent blend of residential and vibrant cultural life.

**Watch-out:**
- Choose quieter side streets to avoid night noise.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Haut Saint - Gilles** — Greener and quieter area with good access to amenities. (_watch-out_: Some streets are lively and noisy late at night.)
- **Chatelain** — Offers a lively vibe with lots of cafes and restaurants. (_watch-out_: Traffic noise can be an issue on busy streets.)

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Access to parks and cultural venues, appealing for families.
- Balanced atmosphere between urban and suburban lifestyle.

**Watch-out:**
- Some areas can feel crowded, particularly during events.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chasse** — Lively neighborhood with good transit connections. (_watch-out_: Higher rents closer to EU quarter and busy roads.)
- **Saint - Pierre** — Offers access to green spaces and community vibe. (_watch-out_: Traffic can be congested during peak hours.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
