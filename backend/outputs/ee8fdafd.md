# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family of 3 seeking to buy a 2+ room apartment in Brussels. Budget of €490,000–€895,000. Priorities include cafes, quiet residential areas, and access to parks.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms for family comfort
- Proximity to parks and green areas
- Quiet residential environment
- Easy access to cafes and brunch spots
- Schools within 20 min walking distance

## Nice-to-have
- Community atmosphere
- Close to public transport
- Family-oriented amenities nearby
- Affordable housing options

## Red flags
- High traffic noise near major roads
- Limited availability of family-friendly apartments
- Long commute times to desired schools

## Trade-offs
- Quiet areas may lack vibrant nightlife
- Affordability might compromise location access
- Larger apartments may exceed budget constraints

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ganshoren
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:2 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Offers beautiful parks like Parc Elisabeth for relaxation.
- Great for families due to its overall quiet atmosphere.

**Watch-out:**
- Can feel isolated; fewer amenities compared to larger districts.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: bus-based; verify nearest stops
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Bois du Laarbeek - Poelbos** — This area features extensive green spaces ideal for family outings. (_watch-out_: Smaller commune might lack diverse cafes and brunch options.)
- **Ganshoren Centre** — Well-connected to public transport and local amenities for families. (_watch-out_: Limited nightlife and entertainment options available.)

### 2) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Major cultural and shopping attractions enhance lifestyle choices.
- Easy access to parks allows for family recreation.

**Watch-out:**
- Busy traffic areas can create noise disturbances, especially on weekends.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Rich in cultural attractions and local cafes for family fun. (_watch-out_: Touristy areas can become noisy during weekends.)
- **Bois de la Cambre** — Offers expansive park space ideal for family outings. (_watch-out_: Traffic can be heavy in popular tourist seasons.)

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Excellent transport links and amenities.
- Family-friendly parks and activities.

**Watch-out:**
- Potentially higher living costs due to proximity to the city center.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — Engaging community with decent parks for family activities. (_watch-out_: Higher rents near the EU quarter can stretch budgets.)

## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
