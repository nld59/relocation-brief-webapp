# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family looking to buy a premium home in Brussels. Budget 770,000–1,155,000 EUR. Priorities include childcare access, parks, and a quiet environment.

## Must-have
- 3 or more rooms for family living.
- Access to quality childcare services.
- Residential areas with low noise levels.
- Proximity to parks and green spaces.
- Cafes and brunch options within walking distance.

## Nice-to-have
- Good public transportation links.
- Family-oriented community atmosphere.
- Safety ratings above average.
- Modern amenities in residential buildings.
- Access to reputable schools nearby.

## Red flags
- Properties in high-traffic areas may have noise issues.
- Budgets may limit options in prime districts.
- Long commute times could impact daily routines.
- Limited green areas in densely populated zones.
- Potential for hidden costs in property transfers.

## Trade-offs
- High-end properties may exceed budget constraints.
- Desirable districts may lack family-sized homes.
- Quiet areas may not have vibrant cafe scenes.
- Residential feel could limit access to amenities.

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Close to Cinquantenaire Park, great for family outings.
- Residential areas with quality schools and childcare options.

**Watch-out:**
- Property prices can be high during peak seasons.

### 2) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Features many parks, ideal for children's recreational activities.
- Strong community feel with access to quality childcare.

**Watch-out:**
- Property maintenance costs may be above average.

### 3) Watermael-Boitsfort
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Nearby Bois de la Cambre is perfect for outdoor activities.
- Quiet neighborhoods with excellent local schools.

**Watch-out:**
- Limited public transport options can affect commute.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What is the total cost, including taxes and fees?
- Are there any legal compliance issues to consider?
- What maintenance works are needed for the property?
- What is the condition of the building and any inspections?
- What is the timeline for closing and negotiation?

- Are there any specific features you require in a home?
