# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family of three based in Brussels seeking to buy an apartment. Budget between 620000 EUR and 1065000 EUR, prioritizing cafes, quiet living, green parks, and family-friendly atmosphere.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms
- Access to schools within 20 min walk
- Green parks nearby

## Nice-to-have
- Cafes and brunch places nearby
- Family-friendly community
- Premium feel apartments

## Red flags
- High traffic noise in certain areas
- Potentially high property prices
- Budget stretch in premium neighborhoods

## Trade-offs
- Quiet residential areas may be farther from cafes
- Higher property prices in family-friendly zones
- Walkable areas might limit housing options

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Woluwe-Saint-Pierre
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:3 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:2 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Close to Parc de Woluwe for outdoor activities and relaxation.
- Stockel area features diverse cafes and a welcoming community.

**Watch-out:**
- Property prices vary significantly; some areas may stretch budgets.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Saint - Paul** — Good transit options make commuting to schools easy. (_watch-out_: House-focused pockets can raise overall housing costs.)
- **Sainte - Alix - Joli Bois** — Lively atmosphere suitable for families and young children. (_watch-out_: Fewer nightlife options compared to central locales.)

### 2) Auderghem
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:3 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:2 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Accessible parks encourage family outings and activities.
- Herrmann-Debroux provides good transport options for commuting.

**Watch-out:**
- Suburban nature might make access to cafes less convenient.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: metro access, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chant D'oiseau** — Greener area ideal for family strolls and outdoor play. (_watch-out_: Certain areas may feel more suburban with limited amenities.)
- **Parc de la Woluwe** — Offers great green spaces complemented by local cafes. (_watch-out_: Commute times may vary widely depending on exact location.)

### 3) Ganshoren
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:3 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:2 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Strong fit for your priorities around Parc Elisabeth edge.
- Balanced trade-off between lifestyle and commute.

**Watch-out:**
- Smaller commune; fewer nightlife options, but good for quiet routines.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: bus-based; verify nearest stops
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Bois du Laarbeek - Poelbos** — Greener area that provides ample space for children to play. (_watch-out_: Fewer nightlife options could limit dining choices.)
- **Ganshoren Centre** — Good transit ensures easy access to schools and amenities. (_watch-out_: Limited housing stock in certain family-friendly pockets.)


## Next steps
- Schedule viewings across the 3 communes and keep notes in a single tracker.
- Confirm total monthly cost: rent + charges + utilities (and what's included).
- Test the commute at peak hours (public transport and/or car).
- Ask about parking rules/permits and bike/storage options.
- Prepare a document pack: ID, proof of income, employer letter, bank statements.
- For the top 2 options, do an evening walk-through to assess noise and safety.
- If buying: request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes.
- If renting: clarify contract length, notice period, indexation, and deposit rules.
- Book a second visit with measurements and photos to compare objectively.
- Pre-validate financing (or a rental guarantee) to move fast on good listings.
