# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family with 1 child seeking to buy in Brussels. Budget between 705000 and 1440000 EUR. Priorities include premium feel, parks access, and childcare.

## Executive summary (quick scan)
| Commune | Best for | Watch-outs | Top microhoods |
|---|---|---|---|
| City of Brussels | Stronger green pockets around Grand Place. | Traffic/noise can be noticeable on main arteries; shortlist street-by-street. | Grand Place · Brugmann - Lepoutre |
| Etterbeek | Strong international / expat ecosystem and services. | Prime pockets can be pricey; validate the full purchase cost. | Jourdan · Saint - Pierre |
| Woluwe-Saint-Pierre | Stronger green pockets around Saint - Michel. | Check building charges, EPC, and noise insulation — these vary street-by-street. | Saint - Michel · Chant D'oiseau |

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms minimum
- Access to childcare facilities
- Proximity to parks and green areas
- Commute to school within 25 min walk

## Nice-to-have
- Cafes and brunch spots nearby
- Good public transport access
- Community-oriented neighborhoods
- Family-friendly amenities available

## Red flags
- High noise levels in busy areas
- Limited parking in some neighborhoods
- Potential for budget overruns in premium districts
- Commute challenges in specific locations

## Trade-offs
- Premium feel often means higher rents
- Parks may not always have nearby schools
- Cafes might attract more foot traffic
- Childcare access can vary by neighborhood

## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
