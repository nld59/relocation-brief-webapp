# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family of three looking to buy a house in Brussels, budget of 770,000–1,080,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, parks, and access to the EU quarter.

## Must-have
- Minimum 2 bedrooms and a garden
- Proximity to international schools
- Quiet residential neighborhoods
- Easy access to public transport
- Nearby cafes and green spaces

## Nice-to-have
- Modern kitchen and facilities
- Community with expat families
- Access to farmers markets
- Safety features in the neighborhood
- Nearby recreational areas

## Red flags
- High noise levels in certain areas
- Limited parking availability in the vicinity
- Potential for budget increases in housing market
- Proximity to busy roads could reduce comfort
- Older properties may require extensive renovations

## Trade-offs
- Quiet neighborhoods may limit access to city center
- Proximity to parks may reduce housing options
- New developments might lack residential charm
- Expats areas could be more expensive

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Vibrant cafes along Place Flagey foster a bustling community atmosphere.
- Parks like Parc Tenbosch offer green spaces for relaxation and children's activities.

**Watch-out:**
- Noise from nightlife can disrupt evening peace in certain pockets.

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:4 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Access to EU quarter enhances convenience for expatriates living and working.
- Parc du Prince provides ample space for family activities and relaxation.

**Watch-out:**
- Fast traffic in some streets poses a safety concern for young children.

### 3) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Cultural diversity enhances the community experience with numerous dining options.
- Close proximity to parks allows for family outings and outdoor activities.

**Watch-out:**
- Certain historic buildings may require extensive maintenance.


## Next steps
- Schedule viewings for shortlisted properties.
- Discuss financing options with a mortgage advisor.
- Research local schools and amenities.
- Gather necessary documentation for purchase.
