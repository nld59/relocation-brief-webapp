# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family with a 2-year-old, seeking to buy a 2+ room apartment in Brussels. Budget is EUR 490,000–895,000. Priorities include cafes, quiet residential areas, and parks.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms for family comfort
- Good access to parks and green areas
- Proximity to schools within 20 min walk

## Nice-to-have
- Local cafes and brunch spots nearby
- Quiet residential neighborhoods
- Value for money in property pricing

## Red flags
- High noise levels in busy districts
- Commute lengths exceeding 20 min to schools
- Potentially high maintenance costs for older properties

## Trade-offs
- Quiet areas may be farther from amenities
- Value properties might lack newer features
- Balancing budget constraints with desired locations

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ganshoren
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:2 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Contains numerous parks like Parc Elisabeth, ideal for families.
- Offers a quieter lifestyle with accessible amenities.

**Watch-out:**
- Smaller commune may lack vibrant nightlife and activities.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: bus-based; verify nearest stops
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Bois du Laarbeek - Poelbos** — Offers green spaces with numerous trails for family activities. (_watch-out_: Fewer nightlife options may limit social activities.)
- **Ganshoren Centre** — Good transit options enhance connectivity to the city. (_watch-out_: Limited dining choices could affect meal options.)

### 2) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Offers access to cultural sites and parks near the Royal Palace.
- Strong dining options in a cosmopolitan environment attract families.

**Watch-out:**
- Traffic can lead to noise issues, especially on weekends.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Features parks and good access to public transportation. (_watch-out_: Busy areas may experience high traffic noise.)
- **Haren** — Lively area with amenities nearby for families. (_watch-out_: Tourist crowds can create safety concerns.)

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Accessibility to European institutions and parks.
- Strong fit for your priorities around Jourdan.

**Watch-out:**
- May have higher competition for available apartments.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — Lively area with the charm of local cafes and boutiques. (_watch-out_: High rents near EU quarter may stretch budgets.)
- **Chasse** — Good starting point with good tram coverage. (_watch-out_: Watch for less greenery.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
