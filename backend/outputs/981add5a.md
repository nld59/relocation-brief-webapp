# Relocation Brief — Brussels

## Client profile
Family of three looking to buy a property in Brussels. Budget is EUR 730,000 to EUR 1,090,000. Prioritizing restaurants, parks, expat community, and central access.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms in a family-friendly area
- Proximity to parks and green spaces
- Access to restaurants and cafes
- Good schools within 20 min walk
- Strong public transport connections

## Nice-to-have
- Outdoor spaces for children
- Community activities for expats
- Shopping options nearby
- Cultural attractions within reach
- Quiet, residential neighborhoods

## Red flags
- High competition for desirable properties
- Potentially inflated prices in central areas
- Traffic congestion affecting commutes
- Safety concerns in some districts
- Limited availability of larger homes

## Trade-offs
- Budget may limit access to preferred districts
- Desirable areas may lack larger homes
- Taste for urban amenities vs. residential comfort
- Interest in expat community vs. local integration

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Nearby Cinquantenaire park is perfect for family outings.
- Strong access to restaurants in lively Jourdan area.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near EU quarter can be higher; traffic around main axes.

### 2) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Royal Palace area offers cultural attractions and parks.
- Dynamic restaurant scene around Sablon is family-friendly.

**Watch-out:**
- Touristy pockets and traffic can feel noisy on weekends.

### 3) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Flagey area is vibrant with cafes and parks for families.
- Accessible public transport facilitates easy city travel.

**Watch-out:**
- Popular streets get noisy on weekends; parking is often tight.


## Next steps
- Review potential properties in preferred districts.
- Schedule visits to shortlisted homes.
- Discuss financial options with a local bank.
- Engage with a real estate agent for support.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What are the total costs involved in the purchase?
- Are there any ongoing building charges or planned works?
- How does the property comply with local regulations?
- What is the condition of the property and when can I inspect?
- What is the expected timeline for negotiations and closing?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- Are there any specific school preferences?
- Do you have a preference for new or older properties?
- What is your ideal commute time to work?
