# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple seeking to buy an apartment in Brussels, budget of 490,000–895,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, restaurants, expat community, and access to the EU quarter.

## Must-have
- 1+ room apartment
- Access to cafes & brunch
- Proximity to restaurants
- International community presence
- Access to EU quarter

## Nice-to-have
- Public transport accessibility
- Green spaces nearby
- Local markets and shops
- Cultural activities
- Family-friendly amenities

## Red flags
- Rising rent prices near EU quarter
- Limited parking availability
- Street noise in busy areas
- Potential budget stretch in prime locations
- Long commute times from outskirts

## Trade-offs
- Prefer urban areas but seek quiet streets
- Desire for lively neighborhoods vs. need for peace
- Budget for a premium area but open to other options
- Wanting modern apartments while considering older buildings

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Close proximity to Cinquantenaire park for leisure and outdoor activities.
- Strong metro connections enhance access to the EU quarter and city center.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near the EU quarter can be higher.

### 2) IXELLES
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:3 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Vibrant food scene with numerous cafes and restaurants along Châtelain.
- Cultural events and nightlife cater to an international crowd and expats.

**Watch-out:**
- Popular streets get noisy on weekends.

### 3) Saint-Josse-ten-Noode
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:2 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:4 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Varied dining options and local cafes appeal to an expat lifestyle.
- Good metro access makes commuting to the EU quarter smooth and quick.

**Watch-out:**
- Dense and busy
