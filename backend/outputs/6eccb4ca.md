# Relocation Brief — Brussels

## Client profile
Family living in Brussels, seeking to buy a 2+ room home. Budget is max 700000 EUR. Priorities include EU quarter access, culture, and expat-friendly areas.

## Executive summary (quick scan)
| Commune | Best for | Watch-outs | Top microhoods |
|---|---|---|---|
| Etterbeek | Strong international / expat ecosystem and services. | Prime pockets can be pricey; validate the full purchase cost. | Saint - Pierre · Jourdan |
| Saint-Gilles | Balanced option around Saint-Gilles with a clear trade-off between space, commute and amenities. | Traffic/noise can be noticeable on main arteries; shortlist street-by-street. | Porte de Hal · Gare du Midi |
| Schaerbeek | Balanced option around Schaerbeek with a clear trade-off between space, commute and amenities. | Check building charges, EPC, and noise insulation — these vary street-by-street. | Collignon · Dailly |

## Must-have
- Proximity to EU quarter
- Good access to schools
- Cultural amenities nearby
- Safe environment for children
- Strong public transport connections

## Nice-to-have
- Art and design spaces nearby
- Diverse dining options
- Parks and playgrounds within walking distance
- Community activities for families

## Red flags
- High traffic areas may impact air quality
- Limited parking availability in some districts
- Noise levels during weekends in tourist spots
- Potential for higher property prices near EU quarter

## Trade-offs
- Urban density may conflict with quiet residential need
- Cultural hubs often attract crowds; choose wisely
- Expats may drive up prices in popular areas

**Why:**
- Offers excellent metro access and proximity to parks.
- Welcoming atmosphere for expats and families.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near EU quarter can be higher; consider budget accordingly.

## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
