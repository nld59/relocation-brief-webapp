# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels. Budget is 490,000–1,050,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes & brunch, restaurants, and central access.

## Must-have
- 1+ rooms
- Apartment
- Central access

## Nice-to-have
- Modern finishes
- Balcony or terrace
- Proximity to parks

## Red flags
- High traffic areas
- Limited public transport options
- Excessive noise during weekends

## Trade-offs
- Desire for central access vs. need for quiet
- Restaurants nearby vs. potential noise
- Urban lifestyle vs. budget constraints

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Central access enhances lifestyle with various cafes and restaurants.
- Proximity to cultural landmarks and green spaces enriches daily life.

**Watch-out:**
- Traffic noise can be significant during busy periods.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Offers green space and good transit connections in a lively environment. (_watch-out_: Check: Touristy pockets and traffic can feel noisy on weekends.)
- **Haren** — Lively atmosphere with excellent local amenities and cafes nearby. (_watch-out_: Check: Can be very busy during peak times.)
- **Heembeek** — Strong public transport options connect to downtown Brussels easily. (_watch-out_: Check: Traffic congestion during rush hours can be challenging.)

### 2) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Diverse dining and cafe options enhance community engagement.
- Strong cultural identity with events and vibrant street life.

**Watch-out:**
- Expect higher noise levels in busy areas during late nights.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Greener environment with a lively community and vibrant cafes. (_watch-out_: Check: Popular streets get noisy on weekends; parking is often tight.)
- **Brugmann - Lepoutre** — Rich in dining options and cultural activities for young professionals. (_watch-out_: Check: Can be crowded during evening hours, impacting tranquility.)
- **Universite** — Dynamic area known for its cafes, nightlife, and student presence. (_watch-out_: Check: High demand may inflate rental and purchase prices.)

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Family-friendly neighborhood with schools and parks.
- Variety of shops and local market add to community feel.

**Watch-out:**
- Traffic can be heavy during rush hours.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — Lively atmosphere with vibrant local cafe culture and amenities. (_watch-out_: Check: Can be quite busy during weekend evenings.)
- **Chasse** — Good starting point with good tram coverage. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for less greenery.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
