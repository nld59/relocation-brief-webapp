# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple seeking to buy an apartment in Brussels. Budget is 1,240,000–1,500,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, restaurants, and a quiet residential area.

## Must-have
- 1+ room apartment suitable for couple
- Quiet neighborhood ideal for work from home
- Access to public transportation and amenities
- Proximity to cafes and brunch spots
- Residential area with low noise levels

## Nice-to-have
- Balcony or outdoor space for relaxation
- Modern kitchen with quality appliances
- Fitness center or gym in the building
- Parking space available or easy access
- Close to parks or green spaces

## Red flags
- Limited availability of listings under budget
- Potential for noise in busy districts
- High competition for popular neighborhoods
- Long commute times from certain suburbs
- High property taxes in urban areas

## Trade-offs
- Desire for quiet but in trendy areas
- Balance between budget and quality amenities
- Residential charm vs. urban convenience
- Modern features vs. historic architecture

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Rich cultural scene with cafes around Flagey Square.
- Residential streets provide a calm atmosphere for families.

**Watch-out:**
- Higher property prices due to its desirable location.

### 2) Sablon
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Famous for its brunch spots near Place du Grand Sablon.
- Art galleries and quiet streets provide a unique vibe.

**Watch-out:**
- Busy weekends can lead to noise and crowds.

### 3) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Known for its diverse restaurants near Parvis de Saint-Gilles.
- Residential yet vibrant area ideal for young couples.

**Watch-out:**
- Busy areas may disrupt desired quiet lifestyle.
