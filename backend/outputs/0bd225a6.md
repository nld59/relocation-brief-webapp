# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family looking to buy a home in Brussels. Budget is 715000–975000 EUR. Priorities include residential areas, family-friendliness, and local cafes.

## Must-have
- Minimum of 2 bedrooms for family needs.
- Access to high-quality schools within a 20 min walk.
- Quiet neighborhoods for a peaceful living environment.
- Proximity to family-friendly parks and recreational areas.
- Availability of expat-friendly amenities and cafes.

## Nice-to-have
- Public transportation options for easy commuting.
- Local community events for family engagement.
- Tree-lined streets for better air quality.
- Pet-friendly areas for family pets.
- Cultural venues offering family programming.

## Red flags
- High traffic areas can disrupt the peaceful environment.
- Limited parking options may complicate daily activities.
- School performance variability in some neighborhoods.
- Higher renovation costs in older buildings.
- Limited access to childcare services in certain areas.

## Trade-offs
- Family-friendly areas may have higher living costs.
- Quiet neighborhoods might be farther from city amenities.
- Desirable cafes could lead to noise and traffic.
- Expats' needs may drive up local housing prices.

### 3) Schaerbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What is the required deposit and are there any additional fees?
- Are utilities included in the rental price?
- What is the lease term and termination policy?
- Are there any noise rules or regulations in the neighborhood?
- How is rent indexed or registered annually?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- What type of housing is preferred: apartment or house?
- Is a garden or outdoor space important?
- What are your preferences regarding renovations?
