# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family seeking to buy in Brussels, budget 825000–1050000 EUR. Priorities include residential areas, family-friendly amenities, and nearby parks.

## Must-have
- Minimum of 3 rooms for family needs.
- Proximity to parks and green areas.
- Access to family-friendly cafes and brunch spots.

## Nice-to-have
- Close to international schools.
- Community with expat presence.
- Quiet neighborhoods with low traffic.

## Red flags
- High traffic areas may reduce quietness.
- Limited public transport options could affect commuting.
- Some neighborhoods may be pricier than budget allows.

## Trade-offs
- Family-friendly areas may not have extensive nightlife options.
- Quiet neighborhoods may lack immediate amenities.
- Residential zones might have longer commutes.

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Features beautiful parks like Parc de Woluwe, perfect for family outings.
- Highly rated international schools ensure quality education for children.

**Watch-out:**
- High property prices may stretch the budget significantly.

### 2) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Known for family-friendly cafes and vibrant community for expats.
- Easy access to Parc du Cinquantenaire offers ample green space.

**Watch-out:**
- Busy streets can lead to noise and congestion issues.

### 3) Woluwe-Saint-Lambert
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Quiet residential areas with plenty of parks suitable for families.
- Offers excellent selection of international schools nearby.

**Watch-out:**
- Limited restaurant options may hinder dining variety.
