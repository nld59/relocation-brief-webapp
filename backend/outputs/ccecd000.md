# Relocation Brief — Brussels

## Client profile
Couple in Brussels looking to buy a 2+ room home. Budget is 3500–4500 EUR. Priorities include cafes, quiet areas, and family-friendly spaces.

## Executive summary (quick scan)
| Commune | Best for | Watch-outs | Top microhoods |
|---|---|---|---|
| Etterbeek | Strong international / expat ecosystem and services. | Prime pockets can be pricey; validate the full purchase cost. | Jourdan · Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien |
| City of Brussels | Stronger green pockets around Brugmann - Lepoutre. | Traffic/noise can be noticeable on main arteries; shortlist street-by-street. | Brugmann - Lepoutre · Dries |
| Saint-Gilles | Balanced option around Saint-Gilles with a clear trade-off between space, commute and amenities. | Check building charges, EPC, and noise insulation — these vary street-by-street. | Bosnie · Porte de Hal |

## Must-have
- Minimum 2 rooms for comfort and space.
- Access to cafes and brunch spots local to community.
- Residential areas that offer a quiet environment.

## Nice-to-have
- Proximity to family-friendly parks and recreation.
- Nightlife options for social engagement.
- Mixed vibes with a blend of cultural offerings.

## Red flags
- High noise levels in central locations may disrupt peace.
- Potential budget overruns in premium residential areas.
- Commute times could increase significantly without a car.

## Trade-offs
- Desiring quiet living yet interested in nightlife options.
- Seeking residential areas may limit proximity to cafes.
- Wanting a family-friendly neighborhood may restrict budget.

## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
