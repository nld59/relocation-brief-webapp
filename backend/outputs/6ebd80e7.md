# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple seeking to buy a home in Brussels. Budget ranges from 490000 to 1190000 EUR, prioritizing cafes, restaurants, markets, and culture.

## Must-have
- 2+ room property
- Local market vibe
- Good access to cafes
- Near cultural attractions

## Nice-to-have
- Proximity to parks
- Transit access
- Diverse dining options
- Community events

## Red flags
- High traffic noise
- Rising property prices
- Limited parking availability
- Long commute times

## Trade-offs
- Vibrant nightlife vs. quiet living
- Busy central areas vs. family-friendly zones
- Urban lifestyle vs. green spaces

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Known for vibrant restaurant scenes and local markets around Flagey.
- Has several parks like Flagey Park, enhancing the community feel.

**Watch-out:**
- Expect higher noise levels during peak days and weekends.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Offers a mix of parks and vibrant cafes, ideal for leisurely weekends. (_watch-out_: Check: Noisy streets during weekends; parking can be difficult.)
- **Universite** — Lively area with good transit connections and various dining options. (_watch-out_: Check: Heavy foot traffic can cause delays during peak hours.)
- **Flagey - Malibran** — Famous for its rich cultural scene and local markets, perfect for food lovers. (_watch-out_: Check: Popularity leads to higher rent costs; consider quieter streets.)

### 2) Schaerbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Offers a blend of local markets and cultural venues like theaters.
- Parks like Parc Josaphat provide green space amidst urban settings.

**Watch-out:**
- Safety can vary; always verify the exact block you're considering.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Josaphat** — Home to expansive parks and excellent schools, perfect for families. (_watch-out_: Check specific blocks for safety concerns, especially at night.)
- **Collignon** — Known for its lively atmosphere and easy access to amenities and transport. (_watch-out_: Check: Consider noise levels during weekends in busy areas.)
- **Terdelt** — Features local markets and cafes, balancing urban and community vibes. (_watch-out_: Ensure to check for upcoming development projects affecting tranquility.)

### 3) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Strong fit for your priorities around Sablon.
- Balanced trade-off between lifestyle and commute.

**Watch-out:**
- Touristy pockets and traffic can feel noisy on weekends.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Good starting point with more green space, good tram coverage. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for street-to-street variation.)
- **Haren** — Good starting point with balanced everyday amenities. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for less greenery.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
