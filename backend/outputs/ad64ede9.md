# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels with a budget of 725000–1325000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, restaurants, premium feel, and residential quiet.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms.
- Apartment type.
- Budget within 725000–1325000 EUR.

## Nice-to-have
- Access to cafes and brunch spots.
- Proximity to restaurants.
- Quiet residential environment.

## Red flags
- High traffic in popular areas.
- Potential noise from nightlife nearby.
- Over budget in premium neighborhoods.

## Trade-offs
- Desire for both premium feel and budget constraints.
- Quiet residential areas may lack vibrant dining options.
- Cafes and brunch spots can be in busy neighborhoods.

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Central area with fantastic access to cultural sites like Parc Royal.
- Diverse dining options and vibrant café scenes surrounding Sablon.

**Watch-out:**
- Crowds during tourist season could disrupt weekday living.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Offers greenery and strong transit options nearby. (_watch-out_: Touristy spots can be noisy, especially on weekends.)
- **Quartier Nord** — Lively area with a mix of restaurants and cafes. (_watch-out_: High traffic can affect comfort and noise levels.)

### 2) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Strong brunch culture with numerous cafes and trendy restaurants.
- Artistic vibe with access to cultural hubs and parks.

**Watch-out:**
- Expect potential noise levels in popular dining areas.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Known for green spaces and vibrant cafes in the area. (_watch-out_: Weekends can be noisy due to lively crowds.)
- **Dries** — Features good transit access and lively social scenes. (_watch-out_: Tight parking situations in busy periods can arise.)

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Close to European institutions with family-friendly amenities.
- Rich in parks and recreational spaces.

**Watch-out:**
- Higher property prices near key attractions.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — Lively center with great cafes and easy transport access. (_watch-out_: Traffic can be heavy around main roads.)
- **Cinquantenaire** — Offers parks and green areas, popular for family activities. (_watch-out_: Can be busy during weekends with events.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
