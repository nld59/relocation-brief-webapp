# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family seeking to buy a 3+ room apartment in Brussels. Budget is EUR 785,000–1,120,000. Priorities include a residential atmosphere, access to cafes, and green parks.

## Must-have
- 3+ rooms for family living
- Proximity to parks and green areas
- Quiet residential environment
- Access to cafes and brunch spots
- Walking distance to schools

## Nice-to-have
- Modern amenities in the apartment
- Good public transport connections
- Family-friendly neighborhood
- Nearby playgrounds or recreational areas
- Community services and programs

## Red flags
- High noise levels from nearby streets
- Limited access to parking spaces
- Potential budget overruns during purchase
- Long commutes to family-friendly areas
- Lack of local restaurants or cafes

## Trade-offs
- Quiet vs. vibrant area preferences
- Budget constraints vs. premium feel
- Proximity to schools vs. residential feel
- Desire for amenities vs. noise levels

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Family-friendly atmosphere near Flagey Square offers parks and eateries.
- Parc Tenbosch provides green space ideal for children and relaxation.

**Watch-out:**
- High demand may push property prices beyond budget limits.

### 2) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Parc de Wolvendael offers plenty of green space and recreational options.
- Good reputation for schools within walking distance enhances family life.

**Watch-out:**
- Commute times can be lengthy depending on your exact location.

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Parc du Cinquantenaire provides ample outdoor space perfect for families.
- Good transport links connect residents to central Brussels and schools.

**Watch-out:**
- Possible traffic congestion during peak hours can affect commuting times.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What is the total deposit and are there any additional fees?
- Are utilities included in the rent, and how is maintenance handled?
- What is the standard lease term and are there termination options?
- Are there any noise restrictions or rules for tenants?
- How is rent indexation or registration managed?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- What is the preferred timeline for moving?
- Are there specific schools or institutions to consider?
- Is there flexibility in the budget for ideal properties?
