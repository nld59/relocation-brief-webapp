# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Couple seeking to buy an apartment in Brussels, budget 610,000-1,125,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes & brunch, nightlife, and premium feel.

## Executive summary (quick scan)
| Commune | Best for | Watch-outs | Top microhoods |
|---|---|---|---|
| City of Brussels | Stronger green pockets around Grand Place. | Traffic/noise can be noticeable on main arteries; shortlist street-by-street. | Grand Place · Stalingrad |
| Saint-Gilles | Strongest commute option: above-average connectivity around Saint-Gilles via metro/tram corridors. | Prime pockets can be pricey; validate the full purchase cost. | Porte de Hal · Bosnie |
| Ixelles | Strong international / expat ecosystem and services. | Commute convenience varies; test your door-to-door route at peak hours before committing. | Matonge · Flagey - Malibran |

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms apartment
- Cafes and brunch options nearby
- Vibrant nightlife access
- Premium feel in the neighborhood
- Local markets and shopping

## Nice-to-have
- Green spaces or parks
- Good public transportation access
- Proximity to cultural sites
- Family-friendly environment

## Red flags
- High noise levels from nightlife
- Price volatility in premium areas
- Limited parking in popular districts
- Traffic congestion during weekends

## Trade-offs
- High demand may reduce negotiating power
- Premium districts could overshoot budget
- Noisy nightlife vs. peaceful living

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Vibrant cultural scene with easy access to cafes and nightlife.
- Well-connected public transport culture with a strong metro network.

**Watch-out:**
- Expect high tourist foot traffic during weekends.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Grand Place**
  - Portal keywords: Grand Place, Brussels
  - Highlights: Good starting point with strong metro connectivity, solid schools/childcare density, good café and brunch scene. Watch for busier evenings in lively pockets.
- **Stalingrad**
  - Portal keywords: Stalingrad, Brussels
  - Highlights: Good starting point with strong metro connectivity, solid schools/childcare density, good café and brunch scene. Watch for busier evenings in lively pockets; less greenery; check streets close to parks.

## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
