# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Couple buying an apartment in Brussels. Budget EUR 490k-895k. Priorities: quiet residential streets, 15-minute access to green parks, and walkable cafes and brunch spots.

## Must-have
- Min 1 bedroom, 55+ sqm interior.
- Balcony or terrace 6+ sqm with sunlight.
- EPC rating C or better with recent certificate.
- Quiet street or rear-facing bedrooms away from bars.
- Elevator if above 2nd floor.

## Nice-to-have
- 15-minute walk to a large park like Parc Tenbosch.
- Cafes and brunch spots within a 10-minute walk.
- South or west-facing living room for light.
- Secure bike storage in building.
- Cellar storage 4+ sqm.

## Red flags
- EPC rating E or worse with no costed renovation plan.
- Single-glazed street-facing windows on tram routes.
- Visible damp or mold in cellar or bathroom areas.
- Unclear ownership or leasehold terms instead of full freehold.
- Pending facade or roof works without approved budget.

## Trade-offs
- Quiet side streets may reduce immediate cafe options.
- Top-floor charm can conflict with elevator requirement.
- Large terrace can push price above budget.
- Newer energy-efficient builds may be outside historic cores.

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles (Ixelles - Chatelain Tenbosch)
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Around Parc Tenbosch, tree-lined streets mix boutiques and brunch spots with surprisingly quiet courtyards.
- Tram 8 at Bailli connects quickly to Louise and EU stops while keeping commutes simple.

**Watch-out:**
- Prices near Place du Chatelain can stretch budgets, and parking permits are competitive.

### 2) Woluwe-Saint-Pierre
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:4 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Near Parc de Woluwe, low-traffic avenues offer greenery, jogging paths, and calm residential living.
- Stockel metro at Stockel Station brings fast links, with cafes clustered on nearby streets.

**Watch-out:**
- Commutes into the center can be longer, and some areas lack late-night options.

### 3) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:4 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Around Wolvendael Park, residential streets feel village-like, with bakeries and brunch spots nearby.
- Tram 4 at Heros links to the center, keeping travel simple without heavy traffic.

**Watch-out:**
- Street gradients can be steep, and parking is difficult near Uccle Centre on weekends.


## Next steps
- Secure mortgage pre-approval and buyer costs estimate with notary.
- Shortlist 6-8 streets per district using maps and noise data.
- Book first 6 viewings and request syndic minutes and EPC reports.
- Arrange surveyor for structural check and utilities inspection before offering.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What is the all-in acquisition cost estimate including fees, taxes, and notary?
- Are title, urban planning use, and permits fully compliant with no infractions?
- What are monthly co-ownership charges and any voted or planned works with budgets?
- What is the condition of roof, facade, plumbing, and electrics per recent inspections?
- What timeline do sellers prefer, and how flexible is price for negotiation?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- Do you need a private parking space or is street parking acceptable?
- Which floors are acceptable if there is no elevator above second floor?
- What is your target move-in month and renovation tolerance in weeks and budget?
