# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family in Brussels looking to buy an apartment within 490,000-895,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, family-friendly activities, and access to green parks.

## Must-have
- Minimum of 2 rooms
- Parking availability
- Proximity to public transportation

## Nice-to-have
- Balcony or outdoor space
- Nearby schools and childcare
- Access to recreational areas

## Red flags
- High noise levels in tourist areas
- Limited availability of larger apartments
- Fluctuating property prices in popular districts

## Trade-offs
- More family-friendly areas may have higher prices
- Parks nearby can mean more foot traffic
- Central locations can lack quiet residential feel

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Close to Cinquantenaire park for outdoor activities.
- Strong schools and childcare options appeal to families.

**Watch-out:**
- Traffic congestion can affect daily commutes.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — Lively area with family-friendly cafes and parks nearby. (_watch-out_: Check: Higher rents near EU quarter may stretch your budget.)
- **Saint - Pierre** — Has good transit access and vibrant local culture. (_watch-out_: Check: Traffic around main axes can be challenging.)

### 2) Woluwe-Saint-Pierre
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Offers quiet residential living with park access.
- Family-friendly neighborhood focused on community.

**Watch-out:**
- More costly for house-focused areas could strain the budget.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Sainte - Alix - Joli Bois** — Lively area with parks and family-friendly amenities. (_watch-out_: Check: House-focused pockets may be more expensive.)
- **Saint - Paul** — Good transit links make commuting easier for families. (_watch-out_: Check: Fewer nightlife options may limit social activities.)

### 3) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- City's cultural and historical attractions are nearby.
- Access to dining and entertainment options is plentiful.

**Watch-out:**
- Higher living costs in popular neighborhoods.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Greener area with good transit and family-friendly environment. (_watch-out_: Check: Touristy pockets can be noisy on weekends.)
- **Haren** — Offers lively community feel and proximity to parks. (_watch-out_: Check: Tourist areas can create noise issues.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
