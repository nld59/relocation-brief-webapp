# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family buying a house in Brussels with a budget of 490,000–895,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, parks, and family-friendly areas.

## Must-have
- Minimum 2 rooms in a quiet residential area
- Proximity to parks and green spaces
- Family-friendly neighborhood
- Access to international cafes and brunch spots
- Good schools within 20 min walk

## Nice-to-have
- Cycling paths nearby
- Community events and activities
- Easy access to public transport
- Family-oriented activities and playgrounds
- Diverse expat community

## Red flags
- High property prices in certain neighborhoods
- Limited parking availability in busy areas
- Noise from busy streets
- Higher costs in up-and-coming districts
- Long commute times in lesser-known areas

## Trade-offs
- Parks vs. urban amenities like restaurants
- Quiet residential streets vs. vibrant cafes
- Access to public transport vs. traffic congestion
- Family-friendly needs vs. desired expat community

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Canal area offers vibrant cafes and cultural spots.
- Nearby Flagey Square hosts community events and food markets.

**Watch-out:**
- Keep an eye on rising property prices causing budget constraints.

### 2) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Green spaces like Parc de Wolvendael provide outdoor activities.
- Access to top-rated international schools suitable for families.

**Watch-out:**
- Commute times may increase during rush hours due to traffic.

### 3) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Cafes and brunch spots flourish in the charming streets.
- Strong expat community fosters a welcoming environment.

**Watch-out:**
- Be aware of occasional noise from lively nightlife.


## Next steps
- Schedule viewings for top properties.
- Research schools and their proximity.
- Assess local amenities and services.
- Review current market trends and prices.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What are the total costs including taxes and fees?
- Is there a maximum lease period or termination clause?
- Are utilities included in the rent or purchase?
- What is the condition of the property?
- Are there any noise restrictions or regulations?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- What is the preferred move-in date?
- Is flexibility in budget possible?
- Are there specific neighborhoods you prefer?
