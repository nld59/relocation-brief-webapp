# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple looking to buy a home in Brussels with a budget of 490000–1190000 EUR. Priorities include cafes & brunch, restaurants, and a local market vibe.

## Must-have
- 2 or more rooms
- Cafes & brunch nearby
- Access to restaurants
- Local market within 15 min

## Nice-to-have
- Cultural sites close by
- Public transport access
- Outdoor spaces or parks
- Good schools for future children

## Red flags
- High residential noise levels
- Traffic congestion in peak hours
- Lower budget options may lack amenities
- Potential safety issues in some areas

## Trade-offs
- Desire for vibrant areas vs. need for quiet living
- Cafes/bars within walking distance vs. noise tolerance
- High culture access vs. budget constraints
- Seeking local market vibe vs. urban density

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Schaerbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Proximity to Parc Josaphat provides green space for relaxation.
- Mixed residential vibes attract families and young professionals.

**Watch-out:**
- Some blocks may experience higher noise levels or traffic.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Dailly** — Offers lively atmosphere and excellent transit access for commuters. (_watch-out_: Very street-by-street; check safety around your exact block.)
- **Collignon** — Vibrant with cafes and restaurants, ideal for socializing. (_watch-out_: Noise levels can vary by street; check specific locations.)

### 2) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Centrally located for easy access to cultural landmarks and dining.
- Vibrant nightlife within walking distance enhances local experience.

**Watch-out:**
- Weekend crowds can lead to noise and congestion in busy areas.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Greener area with good transit options for commuting. (_watch-out_: Check: Touristy pockets can feel noisy, especially on weekends.)
- **Haren** — Lively district with a wide range of local dining options. (_watch-out_: Check: Traffic congestion can affect accessibility during peak hours.)

### 3) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Strong fit for your priorities around Châtelain.
- Balanced trade-off between lifestyle and commute.

**Watch-out:**
- Popular streets get noisy on weekends; parking is often tight.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Offers green space and a more tranquil environment. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for street-to-street variation.)
- **Brugmann - Lepoutre** — Good starting point with more green space, good tram coverage. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for street-to-street variation.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
