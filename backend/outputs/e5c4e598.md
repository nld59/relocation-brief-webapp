# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family seeking to buy in Brussels, budget 770,000–1,155,000 EUR. Priorities include premium feel, childcare access, and quiet residential areas.

## Must-have
- 3+ rooms suitable for family living
- Access to quality childcare options
- Proximity to parks and green areas
- Quiet residential environment
- Cafes and brunch establishments nearby

## Nice-to-have
- Good public transport connections
- Family-oriented community
- Nearby recreational facilities
- Proximity to schools and educational facilities
- Modern amenities

## Red flags
- High competition in desirable districts may affect availability
- Unexpected costs associated with property maintenance
- Potential noise from nearby locations
- Limited parking options in certain areas
- Rising property prices may exceed budget

## Trade-offs
- Desire for quiet areas may limit access to lively cafes
- Premium properties may come with higher maintenance costs
- Preference for walkability may reduce options for large homes
- Childcare proximity could clash with budget constraints

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Features Flagey square, known for its cafes and cultural vibe.
- Offers access to Parc Tenbosch, a green oasis for families.

**Watch-out:**
- Parking can be challenging due to local events and cafes.

### 2) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Situated near beautiful parks, ideal for children's playtime.
- Housing options include family-friendly homes near schools.

**Watch-out:**
- High demand may push property prices beyond budget.

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Adjacent to Parc du Cinquantenaire, great for outdoor activities.
- Offers convenient access to local markets and brunch spots.

**Watch-out:**
- Commuting may take longer during peak traffic times.


## Next steps
- Research neighborhood specifics and amenities.
- Visit potential properties in shortlisted districts.
- Engage with local real estate agencies for more options.
- Review financial and legal documentation requirements.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What are the total costs including taxes and fees?
- Are there any specific legal restrictions for the property?
- What are the building charges and recent maintenance works?
- What is the condition of the property and any necessary inspections?
- What is the expected timeline for negotiations and finalizing purchase?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- What specific school requirements should be considered?
- Are there preferences for new vs. older properties?
- What is the expected move-in timeline?
