# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels, budget of 490000–1050000 EUR. Priorities include cafes & brunch, restaurants, and central access.

## Must-have
- 1+ bedroom apartment
- Close to public transit
- Access to cafes and restaurants

## Nice-to-have
- Outdoor space
- Modern finishes
- Parking availability

## Red flags
- High noise levels in tourist areas
- Over budget in popular districts
- Long commute times from outskirts

## Trade-offs
- Desire for central access vs. budget constraints
- Preference for modern style vs. older buildings
- Need for noise reduction vs. urban lifestyle

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Central area offers diverse cafes and cultural attractions.
- Excellent access to metro stations and bus routes.

**Watch-out:**
- High noise levels during weekends from tourist activity.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Features green spaces like Royal Park and easy transit connections. (_watch-out_: Check: Touristy pockets and traffic can feel noisy on weekends.)
- **Haren** — A lively area with good transport access and local amenities. (_watch-out_: Check: Can experience high rental competition during peak seasons.)

### 2) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Popular for its cafes and nightlife scene, ideal for young professionals.
- Central location connects easily to public transit systems.

**Watch-out:**
- Parking can be a challenge, especially on busy streets.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Offers a mix of green spaces and lively markets. (_watch-out_: Check: Popular streets get noisy on weekends; parking is often tight.)
- **Dries** — A vibrant area with a mix of local shops and cafes. (_watch-out_: Check: Potentially high foot traffic during nightlife hours.)

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Family-friendly atmosphere with various amenities.
- Close to green spaces and local transport.

**Watch-out:**
- Busy roads can lead to congestion.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — Offers great transport links and is family-friendly. (_watch-out_: Check: Rents near EU quarter can be higher; traffic around main axes.)
- **Saint - Pierre** — A vibrant neighborhood with access to parks. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for street-to-street variation.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
