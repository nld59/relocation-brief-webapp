# Relocation Brief — Brussels

## Client profile
Couple relocating to Brussels, looking to buy an apartment priced 790,000–1,260,000 EUR. Priorities include restaurants, cafes, quiet residential areas, and value for money.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms apartment
- Quiet residential area
- Proximity to restaurants
- Good transportation access
- Safe neighborhood

## Nice-to-have
- Access to cafes
- Green spaces nearby
- Modern amenities
- Community feel
- Parking availability

## Red flags
- High competition in the market
- Rising neighborhood rents
- Loud nightlife in specific areas
- Difficult parking in certain streets
- Potential hidden costs in purchasing

## Trade-offs
- Quiet neighborhoods may limit dining options
- Higher safety areas often come with higher prices
- Value for money might mean sacrificing space
- Remote work may reduce priority on commute

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:3 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Châtelain is vibrant, filled with cafes and restaurants catering to diverse tastes.
- Flagey hosts vibrant markets and lovely green spaces perfect for relaxation.

**Watch-out:**
- Popular streets can become noisy during weekends, complicating parking.

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:3 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Jourdan district offers a mix of cafes and restaurants, ideal for modern living.
- Cinquantenaire park enhances community life with recreational spaces for families.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near EU quarter tend to be higher; watch for budget impact.

### 3) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:2 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Parvis de Saint-Gilles is known for its bustling cafes and artistic vibe.
- Barriere area maintains proximity to vibrant nightlife while offering residential peace.

**Watch-out:**
- Some streets can be lively and noisy late into the night.


## Next steps
- Schedule property viewings in top districts.
- Gather financial documents for purchasing.
- Consult with local real estate agents.
- Review potential properties and shortlist favorites.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What is the total cost of purchasing the property?
- Are there any pending building charges or works?
- What is the current condition of the property?
- What is the estimated timeline for purchasing?
- Are there any legal compliance issues to consider?

- Are there specific amenities you prioritize?
