# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family looking to buy an apartment in Brussels. Budget is 700000–1205000 EUR, prioritizing residential areas, parks, and cafes.

## Must-have
- Minimum 2 bedrooms
- Proximity to quality schools
- Access to parks and green areas
- Quiet residential environment
- Cafes and brunch options nearby

## Nice-to-have
- Balcony or outdoor space
- Modern amenities
- Secure building access
- Walking distance to public transport

## Red flags
- High noise levels in certain areas
- Limited parking availability
- Proximity to major roads
- Expensive property taxes in some districts

## Trade-offs
- High prices in premium areas might limit space
- Quiet neighborhoods may be farther from cafes
- Family-friendly areas may have high demand

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Offers a blend of residential comfort and lively cafes.
- Parks nearby encourage outdoor activities for families.

**Watch-out:**
- Expect higher living costs near popular cafes and parks.

**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Flagey** — Flagey features a vibrant café scene surrounding the iconic lake. It's a great area for families with activities available. (_watch-out_: Community events can lead to high noise levels during weekends.)
- **Parc Tenbosch** — Parc Tenbosch provides ample green space, ideal for children and outdoor activities. Family-friendly environment is prevalent. (_watch-out_: Limited public transport options may extend commute times.)
- **Avenue Louise** — Avenue Louise is known for its premium shopping and dining options. It offers a lively atmosphere with accessible amenities. (_watch-out_: High traffic can affect the tranquility of living.)

### 2) Saint-Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** —

- Offers a balance between urban life and family-friendly spaces.

- Higher noise levels in busy areas.

**Microhoods to start with:**

- **Parc de la Petite Senne** — Offers a serene environment with family amenities and playgrounds. Good access to park activities for kids. (_watch-out_: Some apartments can be older with potential maintenance issues.)
- **Place Maurice** — Diverse range of cafes enhances local lifestyle options. Great for family brunch outings and social gatherings. (_watch-out_: Parking can be limited in busy brunch hours.)


## Next steps
- Schedule viewings across the 3 communes and keep notes in a single tracker.
- Confirm total monthly cost: rent + charges + utilities (and what's included).
- Test the commute at peak hours (public transport and/or car).
- Ask about parking rules/permits and bike/storage options.
- Prepare a document pack: ID, proof of income, employer letter, bank statements.
- For the top 2 options, do an evening walk-through to assess noise and safety.
- If buying: request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes.
- If renting: clarify contract length, notice period, indexation, and deposit rules.
- Book a second visit with measurements and photos to compare objectively.
- Pre-validate financing (or a rental guarantee) to move fast on good listings.
