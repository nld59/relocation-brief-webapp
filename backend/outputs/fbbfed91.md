# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family of three seeking to buy a quiet apartment in Brussels, budget 475k-1.34M EUR. Priorities include childcare access and cafes.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms apartment
- Childcare access
- Residential quiet area
- Good nearby cafes
- Access to local markets

## Nice-to-have
- Proximity to parks
- Public transport nearby
- Bike-friendly area
- Community events

## Red flags
- High noise levels in popular areas
- Traffic congestion during peak hours
- Limited availability of desired apartments
- Budget constraints in specific districts

## Trade-offs
- Quiet neighborhoods may limit vibrant lifestyle
- Premium feel can stretch the budget
- Accessibility may vary street-by-street
- Rent vs buy may affect location choices

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Schaerbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Family-friendly parks like Parc Josaphat enhance outdoor activities and community feel.
- Local cafes and markets create a vibrant neighborhood atmosphere.

**Watch-out:**
- Street noise can vary significantly; some areas are quieter.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Dailly** — Dailly features lively restaurants and cafes, ideal for families seeking amenities. (_watch-out_: Very street-by-street; check safety and quietness around your exact block.)
- **Collignon** — Great public transport links provide easy access to schools and parks. (_watch-out_: Check: Be cautious about budget stretches in sought-after pockets.)

### 2) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Châtelain market provides fresh produce and a local vibe, perfect for families.
- Numerous childcare options are available, easing parental concerns.

**Watch-out:**
- Traffic can be heavy, especially during weekdays; plan commutes carefully.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Boondael offers green spaces and cafes, excellent for family outings. (_watch-out_: Check: Popular streets get noisy on weekends; parking is often tight.)
- **Dries** — Dries is known for its vibrant market scene and community activities. (_watch-out_: Some streets are busier than others; check specific locations carefully.)

### 3) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Strong fit for your priorities around Jourdan.
- Balanced trade-off between lifestyle and commute.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near EU quarter can be higher; traffic around main axes.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — Good starting point with good metro access. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for street-to-street variation.)
- **Chasse** — Good starting point with good tram coverage. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for less greenery.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
