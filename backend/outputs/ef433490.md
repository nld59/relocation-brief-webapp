# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family in Brussels looking to buy an apartment with budget of 745,000–1,205,000 EUR. Priorities include residential areas, family-friendliness, green parks, and cafes.

## Must-have
- 3+ rooms in the apartment
- Family-friendly environment
- Proximity to parks
- Schools within a 20 min walk

## Nice-to-have
- Cafes and brunch options nearby
- Quiet residential streets
- Good public transport access
- Community vibe

## Red flags
- High competition in family-friendly areas
- Limited availability of larger apartments
- Price fluctuations in parks-adjacent zones
- Potentially high maintenance costs

## Trade-offs
- Family-oriented areas may exceed budget
- Quiet zones can lack amenities
- Parks may not be close to transport
- Expansive layouts lead to higher prices

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Woluwe-Saint-Pierre
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:3 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Access to Parc de Woluwe provides ample green space for children.
- Stockel offers multiple cafes and a community feel ideal for families.

**Watch-out:**
- Some pockets are expensive; budget planning is crucial.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Saint - Paul** — Vibrant area close to Stockel, offers good transport options. (_watch-out_: Higher price per square meter in desirable sections.)
- **Sainte - Alix - Joli Bois** — Lively neighborhood known for parks and cafes, ideal for families. (_watch-out_: Limited nightlife may not suit all preferences.)

### 2) Auderghem
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:3 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Proximity to parks is beneficial for family outings and activities.

**Watch-out:**

## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
