# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels. Budget is 490,000–895,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, restaurants, expat community, and central access.

## Must-have
- 1+ bedroom apartments.
- Access to public transport.
- Proximity to cafes and restaurants.

## Nice-to-have
- Access to parks.
- Family-friendly areas.
- Cultural amenities nearby.

## Red flags
- Limited parking availability.
- High property taxes.
- Potential noise issues in busy areas.

## Trade-offs
- Desire for central access vs. need for quiet.
- Cafes & restaurants vs. affordability.
- Cultural life vs. residential comfort.

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Offers a vibrant atmosphere with plenty of cafes around Châtelain.
- Strong expat community ensures diversity and cultural engagement.

**Watch-out:**
- Noise from nightlife can be disruptive on weekends.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Great vibe with parks and cafes near Flagey Square. (_watch-out_: Check: Busy streets can lead to noise during weekends.)
- **Universite** — Lively area with excellent transit access and cafes. (_watch-out_: Check: Rental demand can push prices up quickly.)

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Central access to Brussels' main attractions, including parks.
- Family-oriented amenities and childcare options are abundant.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near EU quarter can be notably higher.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chaussee de Wavre - Saint - Julien** — Lively area-focused hubs with dining and recreation options. (_watch-out_: Check: Traffic congestion can slow down commuting times.)
- **Saint - Pierre** — Excellent transport links with a growing expat population. (_watch-out_: Check: Rents are on the rise near EU quarters.)

### 3) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Diverse cultural scene with plenty of restaurants and museums.
- Strong public transport access to all parts of the city.

**Watch-out:**
- Higher prices in tourist-frequented areas.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Close to parks and cultural attractions while maintaining quiet. (_watch-out_: Check: Touristy sections can become overly crowded.)
- **Mutsaard** — This area offers a mix of culture, dining, and historical sites. (_watch-out_: Check: Traffic noise may disrupt late-night tranquility.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
