# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family of four seeking to buy a 3+ room apartment in Brussels. Budget is 745000–1205000 EUR, focusing on residential areas, family-friendly amenities, and parks.

## Must-have
- 3 or more rooms in an apartment
- Proximity to schools within 20 min walk
- Access to parks and green areas
- Quiet residential environment

## Nice-to-have
- Cafes and brunch spots nearby
- Family-friendly amenities
- Bike-friendly streets
- Public transport access

## Red flags
- High demand areas may exceed budget
- Noisy locations due to traffic
- Long commutes to preferred schools
- Limited nightlife options

## Trade-offs
- Peaceful areas might have fewer amenities
- Green spaces may be far from active zones
- Higher prices in family-friendly districts

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Woluwe-Saint-Pierre
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:3 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Proximity to Parc de Woluwe offers outdoor activities for children.
- Festive atmosphere around Stockel creates a vibrant community feel.

**Watch-out:**
- Some pockets can be pricier; research before committing.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Saint - Paul** — Close to parks and good transit options; ideal for families seeking calm. (_watch-out_: House-focused pockets are pricey; fewer nightlife options persist.)
- **Sainte - Alix - Joli Bois** — Family-friendly with access to green spaces; perfect for young children. (_watch-out_: Popular areas may stretch your budget significantly.)

### 2) Auderghem
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:3 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Access to parks and bike lanes makes it ideal for families.
- Suburban feel provides quiet living while maintaining accessibility.

**Watch-out:**
- Public transport may vary between pockets; plan deployment routes.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: metro access, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chant D'oiseau** — Green surroundings and lively shops; great for family outings. (_watch-out_: Some areas feel suburban; check commute times to your daily anchors.)
- **Parc de la Woluwe** — Offers a spacious environment with multiple playgrounds; perfect for families. (_watch-out_: Distance from city center may impact social options.)

### 3) Ganshoren
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:2 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Strong fit for your priorities around Parc Elisabeth edge.
- Balanced trade-off between lifestyle and commute.

**Watch-out:**
- Smaller commune; fewer nightlife options, but good for quiet routines.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: bus-based; verify nearest stops
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Bois du Laarbeek - Poelbos** — Good starting point with more green space. (_watch-out_: Watch for street-to-street variation.)
- **Jette Centre** — Good starting point with more green space, good metro access. (_watch-out_: Watch for street-to-street variation.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
