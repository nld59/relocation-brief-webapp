# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family of three in Brussels seeking to buy a 2+ room apartment with a budget of 490,000–895,000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, family-friendly amenities, and green parks.

## Must-have
- 2+ rooms apartment
- Central access to cafes
- Access to parks

## Nice-to-have
- Good schools nearby
- Childcare facilities
- Family-friendly community

## Red flags
- High traffic noise levels
- Limited green space options
- Overbudget listings

## Trade-offs
- Desire for green parks vs urban density
- Family-friendly vs bustling nightlife

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Central location boosts access to parks and cafes.
- Rich cultural offerings enhance family lifestyle opportunities.

**Watch-out:**
- Touristy pockets and traffic can feel noisy on weekends.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Haren** — Lively area near good public transport options. (_watch-out_: Check: Touristy pockets can feel noisy on weekends.)
- **Bois de la Cambre** — Close proximity to green spaces and family-friendly parks. (_watch-out_: Check: Traffic can be a concern during peak hours.)

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Strong community vibe attracts family-friendly amenities.
- Good parks enhance day-to-day outdoor opportunities.

**Watch-out:**
- Rents near EU quarter can be higher; traffic around main axes.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Saint - Pierre** — Lively area with access to transport and parks. (_watch-out_: Check: Higher rents are common around main streets.)
- **Chasse** — Good transit options make commuting simpler and faster. (_watch-out_: Check: Traffic around main axes can create congestion.)

### 3) Woluwe-Saint-Pierre
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:4 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Quiet neighborhoods provide excellent family living options.
- Strong fit for your priorities around Stockel.

**Watch-out:**
- House-focused pockets are pricey; fewer nightlife options nearby.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Saint - Paul** — Good transport links connect residents to the city. (_watch-out_: Check: House-focused pockets can be pricey.)
- **Sainte - Alix - Joli Bois** — Family-friendly area with access to beautiful parks. (_watch-out_: Check: Fewer nightlife options may be limiting.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
