# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels. Budget between 490000 and 895000 EUR. Priorities include cafes, restaurants, expat community, and central access.

## Must-have
- 1+ bedroom apartment
- Proximity to cafes and restaurants
- Access to public transport

## Nice-to-have
- Balcony or outdoor space
- Modern amenities
- Quiet neighborhood
- Expats community nearby

## Red flags
- High renovation costs
- Excessive noise levels
- Overpriced listings
- Hidden fees in transactions

## Trade-offs
- Desire for central location vs. quiet area
- Modern amenities vs. historic buildings
- Expats community vs. local culture

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Strong cafes and restaurants like those around Flagey Square.
- Art and culture attract a vibrant expat community.

**Watch-out:**
- Budget stretch possible during peak property seasons.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Universite** — Lively area with good transit and a vibrant expat scene. (_watch-out_: Check: Popular streets get noisy on weekends; parking can be challenging.)
- **Boondael** — Offers greener spaces and a lively atmosphere for young professionals. (_watch-out_: Check: Some spots are busier at night; noise might be an issue.)

### 2) Etterbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:3 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Proximity to cafes along Jourdan Street enhances lifestyle.
- Schools and parks suit family needs in the area.

**Watch-out:**
- Traffic congestion can affect commute times during peak hours.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center, EU quarter
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Chasse** — Great transit connections make this area very accessible. (_watch-out_: Check: Higher rents near EU quarter can affect budget plans.)
- **Saint - Pierre** — Offers various parks and a lively local dining scene. (_watch-out_: Check: Traffic can get heavy around main thoroughfares.)

### 3) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Cultural attractions and historical sites enhance appeal.
- Vibrant nightlife and diverse communities support expat integration.

**Watch-out:**
- High property prices may limit options.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Quartier Nord** — Diverse dining options available and lively atmosphere for expats. (_watch-out_: Check: Touristy areas can get crowded, impacting comfort.)
- **Haren** — Well-connected and offers a mix of urban and green spaces. (_watch-out_: Check: Tourist traffic can create noise and congestion.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
