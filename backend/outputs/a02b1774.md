# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A family of three seeks to buy a home in Brussels, budget 715,000–975,000 EUR. Priorities include residential areas, family-friendliness, and access to cafes.

## Must-have
- 2+ bedrooms with a spacious layout
- Access to quality schools within 20 min walk
- Quiet and safe neighborhoods suitable for kids
- Proximity to family-friendly cafes and parks
- Good public transport options nearby

## Nice-to-have
- Outdoor spaces like gardens or balconies
- Community activities for families and children
- Expats and international community presence
- Access to recreational facilities and playgrounds
- Local grocery and convenience stores

## Red flags
- High noise levels in busy districts
- Availability of affordable schools may be limited
- Potential for lengthy commutes in specific areas
- Budgets may restrict options in prime locations
- Lack of available properties fitting criteria

## Trade-offs
- Desire for quiet yet wish to live close to cafes
- Need for schools or childcare within walking distance against budget limits
- Family-friendly areas may feel isolated from expat communities
- Expats may not find affordable homes close to parks

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Features parks like Place Flagey, popular for families and activities.
- Offers diverse cafes and shops, fostering a vibrant expat atmosphere.

**Watch-out:**
- High property prices may exceed budget in prime areas.

### 2) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Known for quiet residential streets with quality schools nearby.
- Includes beautiful parks such as Parc de Woluwe for family activities.

**Watch-out:**
- Longer commutes could be necessary for city center jobs.

### 3) St. Gilles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:4 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Residential neighborhoods with a growing expat community.
- Close to cultural spots and local cafes for family outings.

**Watch-out:**
- Some areas may experience high noise levels from nightlife.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What is the total cost including taxes and fees?
- Can you provide details on the property condition and any recent works?
- What is the typical duration and terms for the lease?
- How is indexation managed on the rental agreements?
- Are there any rules related to noise or community guidelines?
