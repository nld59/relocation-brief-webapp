# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
A couple looking to buy an apartment in Brussels. Budget is EUR 610,000–1,135,000. Priorities include cafes, restaurants, and local markets.

## Must-have
- 1+ room apartment
- Proximity to cafes and restaurants
- Access to local markets

## Nice-to-have
- Outdoor space or balcony
- Public transport accessibility
- Cultural venues nearby

## Red flags
- High purchase costs in certain districts
- Noise in tourist areas
- Traffic congestion during peak hours

## Trade-offs
- Desire for local vibe but avoiding tourist noise
- Preferring lively areas while needing quiet nights
- Budget constraints vs premium property expectations

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:4 | Commute:2 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:2 | Overall:3

**Why:**
- Rich cafes and restaurant scene around Châtelain.
- Excellent public transport access via Brussels Metro.

**Watch-out:**
- Expect property prices to fluctuate during high demand seasons.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Higher cost; budget may feel tight in prime streets.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Boondael** — Boondael offers lush green parks and a vibrant atmosphere. (_watch-out_: Check: Popular streets can become noisy during weekends.)
- **Flagey - Malibran** — Flagey is known for its lively markets and cultural events. (_watch-out_: Check: Parking can be difficult to find in crowded areas.)
- **Universite** — Universite features excellent public transport links and local cafes. (_watch-out_: Check: Busy streets may face noise issues during rush hours.)

### 2) Schaerbeek
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:5 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Parc Josaphat provides a lovely green space for relaxation.
- Vibrant local markets and lunch options enhance community feel.

**Watch-out:**
- Street variability requires checking individual safety ratings.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Better value vs central premium areas; more space for the budget.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: depends on address; check travel times
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Dailly** — Dailly is vibrant, offering shops and excellent transport links. (_watch-out_: Check: Safety can vary significantly from street to street.)
- **Collignon** — Collignon is lively with a strong market and community vibe. (_watch-out_: Check: Some streets are busier, increasing noise levels.)
- **Gare Josaphat** — Gare Josaphat benefits from strong public transport connections. (_watch-out_: Check: Keep an eye on potential traffic concerns.)

### 3) City of Brussels
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:2 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:5 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Strong fit for your priorities around Sablon.
- Balanced trade-off between lifestyle and commute.

**Watch-out:**
- Touristy pockets and traffic can feel noisy on weekends.


**Priorities snapshot:**
- Typical housing cost: Mid-range pricing; specific streets vary a lot.
- Public transport: metro access, tram coverage, near train links
- Commute access: city center
- Schools & family: generally family-friendly; parks/schools are a key advantage


**Microhoods to start with:**
- **Domaine Royal Laeken** — Good starting point with more green space, good tram coverage. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for street-to-street variation.)
- **Haren** — Good starting point with balanced everyday amenities. (_watch-out_: Check: Watch for less greenery.)


## Next steps
- Shortlist 8–12 listings across the 3 communes and set up viewings.
- Confirm total purchase cost: price + notary/registration fees + recurring charges.
- Validate commute: run one test route at peak hours (public transport and by car if relevant).
- Ask about parking (permit vs private spot), storage, and building rules.
- Prepare a document pack for offers: ID, proof of funds/pre-approval, and key questions for the seller.
- Request EPC, urbanism/permit docs, and recent syndic/HOA minutes before committing.
- Do an evening walk-through for your top 2 choices to assess noise and street feel.
- Plan your notary steps and financing timeline; align deed date with your move plan.
- Book a second visit with measurements/photos to compare objectively.
- If needed, line up a survey/technical inspection for building issues.
