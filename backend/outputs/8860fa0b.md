# Relocation Brief — Brussels, Belgium

## Client profile
Family seeking to buy in Brussels, budget 825000–1050000 EUR. Priorities include residential quietness, family-friendly areas, and access to cafes.

## Must-have
- Minimum 3 bedrooms for family living.
- Proximity to schools within a 20 min walk.
- Green parks and recreational areas nearby.
- Residential neighborhoods with low noise levels.
- Access to expat community and international services.

## Nice-to-have
- Cafes and brunch spots within walking distance.
- Public transportation options for easy commuting.
- Family-friendly recreational facilities and play areas.
- Outdoor markets and local shops nearby.

## Red flags
- High noise levels in central areas may disrupt family life.
- Potential budget stretch for desired large properties.
- Long commute durations could affect daily family activities.
- Limited availability of family-oriented housing options.
- High competition for homes can lead to bidding wars.

## Trade-offs
- Property size versus budget may limit options.
- Desired quiet areas may lack urban amenities.
- Family-friendly neighborhoods may not have vibrant cafes.
- Proximity to schools versus liveliness of the area.

## Top-3 areas (shortlist)
### 1) Ixelles
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:4 | Family:5 | Commute:4 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Features beautiful parks like Parc Tenbosch, perfect for families.
- Has a vibrant cafe scene along Avenue Louise for brunch outings.

**Watch-out:**
- Residential streets can become noisy during peak dining hours.

### 2) Uccle
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:5 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Offers ample green spaces, including Parc de Woluwe for children's play.
- Known for its family-friendly environment and community vibe.

**Watch-out:**
- Higher property prices due to demand in quiet residential areas.

### 3) Woluwe-Saint-Lambert
**Scorecard (1–5):** Safety:5 | Family:4 | Commute:3 | Lifestyle:3 | BudgetFit:3 | Overall:4

**Why:**
- Contains playgrounds and parks ideal for young families.
- Great mix of residential calm and essential amenities nearby.

**Watch-out:**
- Commute to central Brussels may require longer travel times.

## Essentials to ask your Real Estate agent
- What is the total cost including all fees and taxes?
- Are there any ongoing building charges or planned works?
- How is the property maintained and what is its condition?
- What are the specific lease terms and termination policies?
- How will rent increases be managed during the lease?

## Clarifying questions (if needed)
- What specific features are you willing to compromise?
- Is there a preferred school in the area?
- How important is public transport access?
